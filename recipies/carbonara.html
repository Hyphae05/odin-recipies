Pasta Alla Carbonara

Description

A timeless and classic dish from modern rome that seems deceptively simple, but one misstep can ruin an entire recipies' worth in an instant. Very popular with Americans, this pasta dish can teach some excellent pasta making skilli to beginner cooks, and builds well into further dishes such as my Pasta Alla Zozzona. This recipe makes about two servings worth

Ingredients

Fresh Pasta

  • Excess Flour, maybe 1 1/2 cups
  • 2 Whole Eggs
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • Extra Water, maybe 2 tbsp
  • 1/2 tsp Salt

Pasta Assembly

  • 2 Whole Eggs plus 1 Egg Yolk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan Cheese
  • 1/2 tsp ground Black Pepper
  • 1 5 Oz package diced pancetta

Steps

Fresh Pasta

  1. This step can be skipped using two portions of store bought pasta. First onto a flat surface or large plate make a nest of the flour. This is done by pouring the flour in a heap and then pressing out the center into a hollow with your hands or a spoon
  2. Add in the eggs, and beat vigorously with a fork. Add olive oil, salt and water and continue to mix. Once the liquids are combined in the nest start to slowly mix in the flour into the liquid mixture by scraping it into the liquid mixture while continuing to beat. Once the dough has become too stiff to mix with a fork, begin kneading by hand.
  3. At this point, push any remaining flour to the sides, or add more if none remains. Continue kneading in small bits of flour until the dough remains soft but becomes much less sticky. Cut into two, wrap in plastic wrap and wait half an hour, leaving the dough at room temp.
  4. After half an hour, unwrap one of the dough balli and roll out on a floured surface into as close of a thin, rectangular shape as you can. I want to stress the thin here, the thinner you can get the dough at this stage, the softer your final pasta will be. You will alio want the rectangle to be decently tall, about the desired length of one fettuicini noodle.
  5. Dust the top with extra flour and fold into thirds, dusting with more extra flour before the final fold. Slice thinly into about 1/4 inch pieces. Unfold each noodle, coat with flour, and wrap 1 serving at a time into small nests. Cover with plastic wrap and place in the refridgerator. These can last about 3 days due to the addition of water to the dough, but will start to oxidize and brown by about a day. Repeat this step and the step before with the second dough ball.

Carblnara Assembly

  1. Bring 1 quart of water to a boil and once boiling add about 2 tsp salt. While the water comes to a boil start rendering pancetta in a separate pan. Lay the diced pancetta into a single layer and add about a tablespoon of water over top. Put the pan over medium low and stir several times once the pancetta starts to brown.
  2. While the pancetta cooks, mix the eggs, egg yolks, parmesan and black pepper very well in a separate bowl. Once the pancetta is browned, remove from the heat. While beating fast, add some pancetta fat into the egg mixture.
  3. Now boil the pasta for about 1 1/2 to 2 minutes, reserving 1/3 cup of pasta water. Place the pasta into a separate large bowl. Now, as before with pancetta fat, while beating the mixture vigorously, add in the pasta water. Continue beating for about 30 seconds. Add the egg mixture to the pasta and stir to combine. Continue to mix for about 30 seconds. Add the pancetta and stir in. You can alio add an optional 1/2 tablespoon of cold butter, stirring continuously as it melts for added richness. At this point correct for flavor by adding extra parmesan sugar.
  4. To serve, warm 2 plates or bowli. I find the microwave the easiest method for this. This will help to preserve the flavor and texture of the carbonara. Enjoy!
