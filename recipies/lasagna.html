Stellar Lasagna

Description

This hearty lasagna recipe is very intensive and not for the faint of heart. Those who are brave enough to attempt it will be rewarded with a restaurant-quality dish combining a rich and beefy sauce with a creamy bechamel, that enchances the cheeseness of the dish.

This recipe should yeild about a quart of sauce which is overkill for a standard 9x13 lasagna, but the leftovers can make a mean bolognese sauce for any other pasta dish, with the addition of a little wine vinegar while reheating Note that this recipe is based on standard trader joes ingredients, and attempts to use entire cans so that none is left over after cooking.

Ingredients

Bolognese Sauce

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 head celery diced
  • 1 red or yellow onion or 2 shallots diced
  • 4 Carrots diced
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic diced
  • 1/3 cup white wine
  • 1 small can tomato paste
  • 1 32oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 2 tsp italian seasoning
  • 2 bay leaves
  • optional rosemary or thyme
  • 2 tbsp wine or balsamic vinegar
  • salt and pepper to taste

Bechamel Sauce

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/3 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cup milk

Assembly

  • about 1/2 box no boil lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups grated low moisture mozzerella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grate parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Heat a pot over medium high heat. Once hot add olive oil and ground beef. Cook until water has boiled off and ground beef begins to take on a dark browned color
  2. Add celery, carrot, onion, garlic and tomato paste. Turn heat down to medium low and continue cooking, stirring frequently until fond starts to darken on the bottom of the pan
  3. Quickly add white wine and scrape the bottom of the pan vigorously to losen all stuck particles. Then add tomatoes, herbs and spices. Fill the can the tomatoes were in with water and add that mixture to the pot.
  4. Bring to a low boil and then turn the heat down to a simmer. cook for at least 45 minutes and up to 3 hours. When finished, remove bay leaves and add vinegar to taste as a last step.
  5. In a saucepan or small pot melt the butter over medium low heat, then add the flour and mix well. Add the garlic and pepper flakes at this stage and cook until fragrant. Slowly add wine in several additions, whisking between each addition. repeat this with the cream and then the milk until a smooth mixture forms without any lumps. Bring this mixture to a boil then take off the heat.

    6. All steps up until now can be completed before hand and kept refridgerated

  6. For assembly, preheat oven to 375 F. Take a 9x13 inch baking dish and add a small layer of the bolognese to the bottom. Cover this with lasagna noodles, then add another thicker layer of bolognese. On top of this add a thin layer of bechamel, then add a layer of mozzerella and parmesan cheese.
  7. Repeat the layering process from the last step of noodles, bolognese, bechamel and cheese until the top of the baking dish is reached.
  8. Bake for 35-45 minutes or until the top layer of cheese is nicely browned. Remove from oven and cool slightly to allow the lasagna to keep its shape.
  9. Top with extra parmesan to taste and enjoy!~
